# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
**CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Task 4 | Megh S. Joshi | 25BAI10178**

---

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do | Why It Matters |
|---|-----|----------------|
| 1 | **Keep your profiles professional.** Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a bio that reflects your academic and career interests. | Recruiters and professors may view your profiles. First impressions matter online. |
| 2 | **Think before you post.** Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?" | Once posted, content can be screenshotted and shared permanently, even after deletion. |
| 3 | **Use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all social media accounts. | Protects your account from unauthorized access and identity theft. |
| 4 | **Give proper credit when sharing others' work** – images, articles, quotes, or videos. | Avoids plagiarism and shows respect for intellectual property. |
| 5 | **Fact-check before sharing news or information.** Use trusted sources and verify claims on platforms like factcheck.in or PIB Fact Check. | Sharing misinformation can damage your credibility and harm others. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Don't | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|----------------|
| 1 | **Don't share personal information publicly** – including your home address, phone number, Aadhaar number, class schedule, or location in real time. | This information can be used by bad actors for stalking, fraud, or identity theft. |
| 2 | **Don't engage in cyberbullying** – no mean comments, harassment, hate speech, or trolling, even as a "joke." | Cyberbullying has serious mental health consequences and may have legal repercussions under the IT Act. |
| 3 | **Don't post about ongoing exams, internal marks, or institutional matters** without authorization. | This can violate university policies and damage institutional reputation. |
| 4 | **Don't click on suspicious links or "free offer" ads** shared via DMs or comments, even from accounts you know. | These are common phishing and scam techniques used to steal credentials or install malware. |
| 5 | **Don't use social media during class or while performing important tasks** without permission. | It affects focus, academic performance, and shows disrespect to instructors. |

Part A – Coding Practice (HackerRank)

Platform used: HackerRank (hackerrank.com)

I created an account on HackerRank and completed the "Solve Me First" challenge
under the Algorithms – Warm Up section. HackerRank is widely used by companies during 
technical recruitment to assess candidates' problem-solving abilities. Getting familiar 
with the platform early in my degree means I will be comfortable using it when I 
begin preparing for internship applications in my second or third year. The platform also
offers structured learning paths and skill certificates that I plan to pursue over time.

---

Part B – Google Workspace Collaboration (Google Forms)

Platform used: Google Forms + Google Sheets (forms.google.com)

I created a five-question Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz using Google Forms.
The form is linked to a Google Sheet that automatically records all responses in a
structured table as soon as someone submits the form. This response sheet can be
used to analyse results, track participation, and identify gaps in digital
awareness among batchmates.

---

How These Tools Will Help Me Academically:

Both platforms demonstrated how digital tools can serve real academic and
professional purposes rather than just being exercises. The Google Form I built
could genuinely be distributed as part of a digital literacy awareness campaign —
it is a functional, shareable resource, not just a practice task. Similarly,
HackerRank is a platform I will return to repeatedly as my programming skills
develop, using it to practice, certify, and eventually demonstrate my abilities to
employers.

# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
**CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Task 5 | Megh S. Joshi | 25BAI10178**

A practical guide for college students in India to protect themselves from cybercrime and identity theft.

---

## General Digital Safety

- [ ] **1. Use strong, unique passwords** for every account (minimum 12 characters, mix of letters, numbers, and symbols). Never reuse passwords across platforms.

- [ ] **2. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all accounts — especially email, social media, and banking apps. Prefer authenticator apps over SMS-based OTP where possible.

- [ ] **3. Never click on suspicious links** in emails, WhatsApp messages, or SMS — even if they appear to come from trusted senders like banks, universities, or government bodies. Always visit official websites directly.

- [ ] **4. Verify the website URL before entering any personal information.** Look for "https://" and a padlock icon. Avoid websites with misspelled domain names (e.g., "sbi-banking.xyz" instead of "onlinesbi.com").

- [ ] **5. Regularly check your bank statements and credit reports** for unauthorized transactions or new accounts opened in your name. Report any anomaly immediately.

- [ ] **6. Do not share personal documents** (Aadhaar, PAN, college ID, marksheets) over WhatsApp, email, or social media unless absolutely necessary and with a verified recipient.

---

## UPI & Financial Safety *(Mandatory)*

- [ ] **7. Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or MPIN with anyone** — including people claiming to be bank representatives, customer service agents, or government officials. Legitimate banks will never ask for these.

- [ ] **8. Be cautious of "receive money" requests on UPI apps.** Scammers often trick users into entering their PIN to "receive" money — entering your PIN on a payment request authorizes a debit, not a credit.

---

## Identity Protection

- [ ] **9. Watermark your documents** (e.g., write "For [specific purpose] only – [date]") before sharing digital copies of Aadhaar, PAN, or college ID to prevent misuse.

- [ ] **10. Log out of all accounts on shared or public computers.** Do not save passwords on public or borrowed devices.

---

## Reporting Channels

If you believe you are a victim of cybercrime or identity theft, report it immediately:

| Resource | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** (24x7, toll-free) |
| **CERT-In** | [cert-in.org.in](https://cert-in.org.in) |
| **Bank Fraud** | Call your bank's 24x7 helpline immediately to freeze the account |

> **Act Fast:** In cases of financial fraud, reporting within the first hour (the "golden hour") significantly increases the chances of fund recovery.

## What is Identity Theft?

Identity theft is a cybercrime in which a perpetrator obtains and uses another person's personal information — such as their name, date of birth, Aadhaar number, PAN card details, bank account information, or login credentials — without their knowledge or consent, typically to commit financial fraud or gain unauthorized access to services.

---

## Case Study: The Case of Riya Sharma (Realistic Fictional Scenario)

Riya Sharma, a 20-year-old engineering student from Bhopal, received an email that appeared to be from her bank. The email warned her that her account would be suspended within 24 hours unless she verified her details immediately. Panicking, she clicked the link, which led to a convincing but fake version of the bank's login page. She entered her customer ID, password, and OTP.

Within hours, the attacker had changed her account password, transferred Rs. 45,000 from her savings account, and used her PAN number (visible in her profile documents) to apply for a credit card in her name.

### How It Happened — Step by Step

1. **Reconnaissance:** The attacker sourced Riya's email address and partial bank details from a data leak on a third-party shopping website where she had an account.
2. **Phishing Email:** A convincing fake email was sent, imitating the official bank communication.
3. **Credential Harvesting:** Riya entered her login credentials and OTP on the fake website.
4. **Account Takeover:** The attacker used the stolen credentials to log into her real bank account and change the registered mobile number.
5. **Financial Fraud:** Money was transferred to a mule account and withdrawn immediately.
6. **Secondary Fraud:** The attacker used her PAN details to apply for a credit card, creating long-term damage to her credit score.

### Who is Typically Targeted?

Identity theft commonly targets college students, senior citizens, and first-time internet banking users — groups that may be less aware of phishing tactics or more likely to act quickly under a false sense of urgency.

### Consequences

Riya suffered immediate financial loss, a damaged credit score, and months of legal and banking procedures to recover her identity. The emotional stress affected her academics, and the fraudulent credit card remained on her record for over a year.
